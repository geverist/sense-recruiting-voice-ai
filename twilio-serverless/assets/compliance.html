⚖️ AI Recruiting Compliance & Ethics

Legal guidelines and best practices for ethical AI interviewing

⚠️ IMPORTANT: This guide is for educational purposes. Always consult with your legal team before implementing AI recruiting tools.

Legal & Regulatory Framework

EEOC Title VII
Prohibits employment discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin. AI systems must not introduce bias.
✓ Avoid questions about protected characteristics
ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)
Cannot ask about disabilities before making a job offer. AI must accommodate candidates who need assistance.
✓ Provide alternative screening methods
ADEA (Age Discrimination)
Protects workers 40 and older from age-based discrimination. Never ask about age or use age as a factor.
✓ Focus on skills and experience, not years
GDPR (EU) / CCPA (California)
Data privacy laws requiring consent, transparency, and right to deletion. Candidates must know they're talking to AI.
✓ Disclose AI use, get consent, protect data
NYC Local Law 144
Requires bias audits for AI hiring tools used in NYC. Annual audits must check for discrimination.
✓ Conduct annual bias audits if hiring in NYC
TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection)
Regulates outbound calling. Must have prior express consent before calling candidates' cell phones.
✓ Get opt-in consent before outbound calls

State-Specific Regulations

📍
California (AB 2551)
Requires notification when interacting with a bot. AI must identify itself at the beginning of the call.
📍
Illinois (BIPA - Biometric Information)
Strictly regulates voice biometrics. Get written consent before collecting voiceprints. Our system does NOT store voiceprints.

Forbidden vs. Allowed Questions

⛔ NEVER Ask These Questions
  • Age: "How old are you?" or "What year did you graduate?"
  • Marital/Family Status: "Are you married?" or "Do you have children?"
  • Race/Ethnicity: "What's your background?" or "Where are you from originally?"
  • Religion: "What church do you attend?" or "Can you work Saturdays?"
  • Disabilities: "Do you have any health conditions?" or "Have you been injured?"
  • Genetic Information: "Does your family have a history of...?"
  • Citizenship (pre-offer): "Are you a U.S. citizen?" (can ask if authorized to work)
  • Arrest Record: "Have you ever been arrested?" (convictions may be OK if job-related)
  • Credit History: Highly restricted in many states unless directly job-related
  • Social Organizations: "What clubs do you belong to?" (could reveal protected class)
✅ Safe & Effective Questions
  • Work Authorization: "Are you authorized to work in the United States?"
  • Experience: "Tell me about your experience with [specific skill]."
  • Skills: "What programming languages are you proficient in?"
  • Availability: "Are you available to start on [date]?"
  • Schedule: "This role requires [schedule]. Can you work these hours?"
  • Salary Expectations: "What are your salary expectations?" (banned in some states - check local laws)
  • Relocation: "Are you willing to relocate to [city]?"
  • Travel: "This role requires 25% travel. Is that acceptable?"
  • Notice Period: "How much notice do you need to give your current employer?"
  • Career Goals: "Where do you see yourself in 3-5 years?"

Best Practices for Ethical AI Recruiting

🤖
Always Disclose AI Use
Your AI agent should introduce itself clearly: "Hi, I'm an AI recruiting assistant calling on behalf of [Company]. This call may be recorded. Is now a good time to chat for a few minutes?"
🔒
Implement PII Redaction
Use Twilio's Language Operators to automatically redact SSN, credit cards, email addresses, and phone numbers from transcripts. This is already configured in your outbound-call-answer.js file.
⚖️
Consistent Evaluation Criteria
AI should ask the SAME questions to ALL candidates for the same role. This ensures fair, unbiased evaluation and protects against discrimination claims.
👤
Human Oversight Required
AI should screen candidates, but humans make final hiring decisions. Never let AI automatically reject candidates without human review.
🎯
Job-Related Questions Only
Every question must be directly related to job requirements. Avoid "cultural fit" questions that could introduce bias.
📊
Regular Bias Audits
Analyze screening outcomes by demographic group (if data is available). Look for disparate impact. If one group advances at significantly lower rates, investigate and fix.
♿
Provide Reasonable Accommodations
Offer alternative screening methods for candidates who can't use voice calls (deaf/hard of hearing, speech disabilities). Consider text-based chat or human recruiter alternatives.
📝
Document Everything
Keep recordings, transcripts, and AI decisions. If challenged, you need to prove your AI acted fairly and legally. Retention: 1 year minimum (EEOC), 3+ years recommended.

When to Escalate to a Human Recruiter

🚨 Escalation Triggers

1 Candidate mentions disability or accommodation needs
→
2 Candidate asks about protected topics (religion, family, etc.)
→
3 Candidate becomes upset or confrontational
→
4 Technical issues prevent fair screening

Escalation Response: "I appreciate you sharing that. Let me connect you with a member of our recruiting team who can discuss this further. You'll receive a call from [Name] within 24 hours. Is there anything else I can help with today?"
